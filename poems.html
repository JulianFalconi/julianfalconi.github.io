WHAT'S THE RUSH?


26, 3
Why isn’t this enough?

Riobamba is where I fell in love with the world.
If ever there was music in my childhood,
I hear it here — where walls flaunt
brick more than paint,
where people work to live
and not the other way around,
where the valley that cradles me,
lying between the static of Chambo
and the roaring weekend cheers of Licto,
nourishes numberless singularities
equally unique and deserving of life.

Gonzalo tells me he lost twenty years.
We dare not count the hours
that rival seeding harvests,
cultivating, and waiting.
Yet, I've learned those hours
are time found and bound
to the sound of a living made hopeful,
by the very ground I left long ago.

I have yet to shoe Oettinger,
carve a path through the thick
between the orange and lemon trees,
find homes for six strays born at my doorstep,
build that fire pit I see lighting the ring of land
behind the workshop,
fish…

By the rushing water of a river moving nowhere fast,
the fourty-something days it takes grass to dull my steps,
and the man who has lost my life's time in his,
what’s the rush?



Wednesday, 3 October 2018

Poems

34 DAYS OF OLD ENTISH

Livings waiting to be sold -
they have forgotten their love
for growing things.
They search for roots
hoping they’ll withstand
those indifferent gasps of their own kind -
circumstantially, fixing on lots to erect themselves upon
as if Probability didn’t forge from something close to nothing.

If only they took more time
to eat earth,
dig deep,
drink water,
and sleep.

Seeds,
periods to forgotten verses,
someone and anyone where the Music of the Ainur
planted an end to a sentence of earth,
the magnitude of all pasts
is today.

"NEVER"


The cry of the heart isn't heard,
it's felt. Sometimes, laying it bare
at the innermost angles of the eyes,
a shine crawls with each breath
that fails to bail the flooding chest.

Oh, how a professed end to "maybe"
can twist a heart and wring out rain
from unspoken words
twixt woven vocal chords.


POEMS


They come and go.
Some, couple and I don’t know
which is which.
Some, light
for the last time before my eyes.
I hone in on the dark,
searching through the abyss of my pupils
for a spark.

But,
through time,
none remain the same.
The words I frame:
fireflies,
romancing in the night.

Thursday, 30 August 2018

TODAY

Livings wait to be sold - we have forgotten how to forge.
Cashing living for livings, we search
for roots that may withstand those indifferent gasps of society.
Circumstantially, we fix on lots to erect ourselves upon
as if Probability didn’t forge from something close to nothing.

Nature,
daughter of Time,
inhales before us;
a cloud ends to begin anew,
a stain rejoices at an excavated smile,
and a steady breeze ventures to feel,
each, moored fragments of wisdom in Her breath.

A seed,
like a period of a forgotten verse, is someone and anyone
where God’s pen planted an end to a sentence of earth,
like the magnitude of all pasts
is today.


PAINTING

The canvas is worked brown
within a frame of green,

sloping veins are etched to be darkened
by glistening voyages of domesticated water,

and paint is buried

to be rendered by strokes of life
and those who know sustenance
in color.
