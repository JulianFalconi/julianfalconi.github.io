PANTAÑO

by Julian Falconi



Dedicated to my friends and family,
whose love’s given wings
to the length of my life.

And to the young,
who must find perspective
in priority.


Pantaño is the small valley where I spent my early childhood and live now.
It is named after a small agricultural village nearby.




WOUNDED

I do not create from pain,
it saps out of me;
I am not the perturbed artist,
I am the steady tree.




BLISS (for Chimborazo)

I wake in Riobamba,
exit the house, and witness:
morning light warm
this rugged Andean quilt,
moist air nourish
the God of Snow,
souls stir life 
into the coming journey…
then the cars.
The fucking cars.




STILL THERE

I used to think Venezuela was fading
under a corrupt government, narcotraffic, 
and a coin torn into millions. 

“I miss it all,” said an engineer
selling coconuts on the side of the road.
“My family, the food, the air, the sun…”

I wondered about its souls,
uprooted and scattered
across these Andean veins:

upon sleeping,
how many still breathe that air, 
beneath that sun?




LOVE, IN HINDSIGHT

I struggled against the tide
as your salty back split the ocean
and the castle crumbled through my fingers.
If only I had turned to face the sunset
beside you, my friend.




SHAME

It’s not the fact.
It’s the electrity
that simmers before,
surges during,
and flows after.

It’s not the fact.
It’s the music;
it’s the song that takes
you and I
and makes us.

It’s not the fact.
It’s the beat my heart beats for
I see seek shelter
from the eyes
of others.




EXPERIENCE

When people hear I’m a college dropout
some express concern,
most look down,
and few look up.
I prefer those who
simply don’t give a fuck.




SUFICIENTE

Me he dado cuenta sobre el tiempo que nos queda
que aunque nunca fue mucho
por lo menos antes
menos
era más.




GOING ON A POEM

I
flipped
the switch
and the lights
remained off. I
told my boss and
she told Mari, who
cleans. She came by,
flipped the switch too
to no avail, but left. Then,
an older man came by, flipped
the switch and also left shadowless.
So,
here I am again, dropping the sails for
another voyage in the dark.
